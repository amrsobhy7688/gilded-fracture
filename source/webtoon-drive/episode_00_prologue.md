# Episode 00 — Prologue: "The Ledger and the Missing 46"

> **Status:** Draft v0.1
> **Function:** Cold open · world-establishing · tone-setter · no protagonist POV
> **Total pages:** 9
> **Total rows:** ~32
> **Estimated read time:** ~90 seconds at average scroll pace

---

## Pacing rationale

The prologue carries the **thesis of the entire series** — *the Empire runs on faith and receipts, and a rounding error is about to break it.* It is dense lore but visually rich, and demands cinematic weight. Nine pages feels right because:

- Pages 1–3 establish **identity and ledger civilization** (the opening hook)
- Pages 4–5 reveal **the Celestial Ledger and the principle of "faith and receipts"** (the worldview)
- Pages 6–7 anchor us in **Cyritium during Festival Week** (the temporal hook)
- Pages 8–9 introduce **forty-six** as a rounding error (the inciting fracture)

Splash panels (R2-TALL, R3-TALL) cluster on **the four highest-weight beats**: the "written in light" reveal, the Celestial Ledger reveal, "faith and receipts," and the closing Cyritium-at-dusk hook. Transitional and atmospheric panels stay smaller (R1-FULL, R1-DUO, R1-TRIO).

## Caption voice

Cold, slightly ironic, almost-religious — like reading aloud from the Celestial Ledger itself. The narrator is the Empire's own voice — *satisfied* (the same tone the Herald uses). No protagonist interiority yet. All text is CAPTION, not dialogue.

Caption styling for lettering pass:
- Standard captions: small, serif, gold-cream tone, soft drop shadow
- Emphasis captions (single-word beats): larger, isolated, more space around them
- Closing captions: slightly faded, almost whispered

## PAGE 1 — "Six Hundred and Forty-Three"

### Row 1 — R1-FULL (banner)
- **Composition:** Wide cinematic establishing shot. Cyritium skyline at dawn from a slight low angle. The High Spire dominates center-frame, catching the first sun. Marble + gold + crystal architecture, painterly Arcane atmosphere with morning mist clinging to lower districts.
- **Lighting:** Cold blue pre-dawn shadows + warm gold sunrise hitting the Spire's peak only. Strong chiaroscuro.
- **Caption (small, top-left):** *Six hundred and forty-three years post-Unmaking.*

### Row 2–3 — R2-TALL (vertical splash, high weight)
- **Composition:** Close-up of a single hand pressed flat against a stone wall. Gold script-like light flows from beneath the palm, tracing the fingers, spilling outward in glowing aether-thread patterns. The "name" written in light, visualized.
- **Lighting:** Black-shadow wall, gold light radiating from the contact point. High contrast. Painterly brush texture visible on the stone and the hand.
- **Caption (top):** *Your name is not written in ink.*
- **Caption (bottom, slightly larger):** *Your name is written in light.*
- **Prompt notes:** hand on rough stone, glowing gold aether script flowing from palm, fingerprint-pattern made of light, Arcane painterly oil-textured rendering, dramatic chiaroscuro

### Row 4 — R1-FULL (banner)
- **Composition:** Extreme close-up — a pair of eyes catching the reflection of a crystal sconce. The reflection in each iris shows the same gold glow. Anime-influenced eye shape, Arcane-style painted skin and lashes.
- **Lighting:** Face mostly in soft shadow, only the eyes catching the warm sconce glow.
- **Caption (overlay, lower):** *To be a citizen of the Cyriath Empire in good standing is to exist inside a ledger so vast, and so precise...*

## PAGE 4 — "The Celestial Ledger"

### Row 1–3 — R3-TALL (full-page splash, **highest weight in prologue**)
- **Composition:** Vertical reveal shot. The Celestial Ledger itself — an enormous bound volume on a stone pedestal in a chamber deep beneath the High Spire. A single shaft of gold light descends from a high oculus and strikes the open page. Dust motes drift in the beam. The chamber walls are unseen, fading into darkness. The book feels older than the city around it.
- **Lighting:** Single beam of gold light. Everything else in deep painted shadow. Arcane chiaroscuro at maximum.
- **Caption (top, small):** *The Celestial Ledger.*
- **Caption (middle, breathing room):** *Maintained in the Office of Holy Order beneath the High Spire...*
- **Caption (bottom):** *...is the longest unbroken document in the known world.*
- **Prompt notes:** massive ancient leather-bound ledger on stone pedestal, single shaft of gold light from above, dust motes in beam, surrounding darkness, gothic chamber, Arcane painterly oil texture, dramatic god rays, awe-inducing scale

### Row 4 — R1-FULL (banner)
- **Composition:** Close-up — a quill pen writing a single line into the ledger. The handwriting is precise, unhurried. Period-correct ink and parchment.
- **Lighting:** Same beam of gold light, now intimate scale.
- **Caption (overlay, upper):** *Six hundred and forty-three years of every crystal that has ever moved through Imperial hands...*
- **Caption (overlay, lower):** *...recorded in the precise, unhurried handwriting of a hundred generations of House Mccord auditors.*

## PAGE 5 — "Faith and Receipts"

### Row 1 — R1-FULL (banner)
- **Composition:** Wide cathedral interior shot. Citizens kneeling in prayer beneath a great stained-glass sun motif. Light pours down. Reverent. Religious.
- **Lighting:** Stained-glass-fractured gold and rose light. Painterly.
- **Caption (overlay, upper):** *The Empire does not run on faith alone.*

### Row 2 — R1-FULL (banner)
- **Composition:** Counter-shot — pulled back to reveal a tax clerk's office. A receipt is stamped and filed onto a stack. Mundane. The contrast with Row 1 is the joke.
- **Lighting:** Flat lamp-amber. Bureaucratic.
- **Caption (overlay, upper):** *It runs on faith...*

### Row 3 — R1-FULL (banner, **emphasized beat**)
- **Composition:** Single sealed receipt being dropped onto the stack. Wax seal visible. The paper lands precisely.
- **Lighting:** Same as Row 2, but the receipt catches a small highlight.
- **Caption (overlay, isolated, slightly larger):** *...and receipts.*

### Row 4 — R1-FULL (banner, **transitional silent panel**)
- **Composition:** Cut to street-level Cyritium. Workers on ladders hanging white silk drapes between buildings. The festival is being assembled.
- **Lighting:** Early afternoon, painterly bloom on the silk.

## PAGE 8 — "Forty-Six"

### Row 1 — R1-FULL (banner)
- **Composition:** The Celestial Ledger again. A clerk's hand turning a page. Same chamber as Page 4, but more intimate framing this time.
- **Lighting:** Same descending gold beam, smaller scale.
- **Caption (overlay, upper):** *You are, by any measure, exactly where you are supposed to be.*

### Row 2 — R1-FULL (banner, **beat panel**)
- **Composition:** Pure close-up on a ledger column. Neat handwritten numbers in rows. Crystal totals. The numbers are mundane, precise. The reader's eye is meant to scan them like a clerk would.
- **Lighting:** Lamp-amber, flat, almost forensic.
- **Caption (overlay, isolated, slightly cold):** *There is only one small thing.*

### Row 3 — R2-TALL (vertical splash, **inciting fracture moment**)
- **Composition:** The same ledger column, but now the number at the bottom is highlighted. The handwritten total has been struck through. A correction is written beneath in slightly different ink. The two totals don't match. The reader's eye is drawn to the difference. **Inset (small, lower-right):** an extreme close-up of the number "46" in the correction.
- **Lighting:** The page is lit cleanly. The discrepancy glows almost imperceptibly — a faint gold shimmer around the corrected number. Subtle.
- **Caption (top, isolated):** *Forty-six crystals are missing from the ledger.*
- **Caption (lower, smaller, dismissive):** *A rounding error. A clerical mistake.*

### Row 4 — R1-FULL (banner)
- **Composition:** The clerk's hand setting the page aside on a small "to file" pile. Already moving on.
- **Lighting:** Flat lamp-amber, indifferent.
- **Caption (overlay, upper):** *The kind of thing that happens when you are operating at this scale.*

## PAGE 9 — "Set Aside"

### Row 1 — R1-FULL (banner)
- **Composition:** Wide pulled-back shot of Cyritium at dusk. The whole city is lit — festival lights coming alive. The High Spire glowing at center. Painterly Arcane sweep.
- **Lighting:** Twilight blue sky giving way to warm city glow. Golden hour bloom on every crystal sconce.
- **Caption (overlay, top, isolated):** *Noted.*
- **Caption (overlay, lower, isolated):** *Set aside.*

### Row 2–4 — R3-TALL (full-page splash, **closing hook — highest weight**)
- **Composition:** The hero shot of the prologue. Cyritium at full festival twilight from a high vantage point — possibly from atop one of the great houses or a lower terrace of the High Spire. The processional route glows golden through the city. White silk drapes catch the light. The cathedral in the distance. The beacon at the High Spire's peak still unlit (it will light on Ascension Day). It is breathtaking. **But:** in the lower-foreground silhouette, a single dark figure stands on a balcony or rooftop, facing away from the camera, looking up at the High Spire. We cannot see their face. They are not part of the celebration.
- **Lighting:** Maximum cinematic — gold city below, blue-violet sky above, the silhouetted figure backlit by the city's glow. Painterly atmospheric depth. Arcane at its most stunning.
- **Caption (top, drifting down the page):** *Not something any reasonable person would pursue when the Empire is in Festival Week...*
- **Caption (mid):** *...every house's compliance report is due, the hum is warm, the silk is draped, the Radiant Broadcast is speaking, the law is absolute, the light is gold...*
- **Caption (large, isolated, breathing room above and below):** *...and everything, by every account ever filed in six hundred and forty-three years of receipts...*
- **Caption (final, small, almost whispered, near the silhouette):** *...is exactly where it is supposed to be.*

## End-of-prologue transition

The prologue ends on the silhouette. Episode 1 opens on a quiet tea house the next morning — a sharp tonal pull from the epic into the intimate. This contrast is intentional and should be preserved in the page-break beat between the prologue and Episode 1.
